## Custom Clearance Process

Basic procedures: accept declaration → review documents → inspect goods → handle tax collection → customs clearance and release.

### 1. Declaration

A custom declaration happens when a unit, enterprise, or individual submits an import-export application to the state-designated agency (**customs**) before an import/export process is completed. After providing the corresponding cargo information and relevant documents, the state-designated agency Customs) for review and approval.

1. **Declaration time.** 

   According to the "Regulations of the Customs of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Import and Export Goods Declaration" and the "Customs Law", the consignee and consignor of import and export goods, and the entrusted customs declaration enterprise shall report to the customs 24 hours before the goods arrive at the customs supervision place. declare.
2. **Declaration documents.**

(1) Basic documents: generally refers to the customs declaration form for export goods, commercial and shipping documents directly related to export goods (usually including invoices, packing lists, contracts)

(2) Special documents: The relevant national laws and regulations stipulate special control documents, mainly including import and export customary licenses, quota licenses and other approval documents. Commodities subject to national statutory inspection and quarantine should submit the inspection certificate issued by the inspection and quarantine agency and the "Exit Goods Customs Clearance Form" stamped with the "Special Seal for Inspection and Quarantine".

### ② Audit documents

After the customs accepts the export declaration, it should review all the documents submitted by the customs declarer. Document review is usually based on the customs declaration form for export goods. According to the relevant national laws and administrative regulations, check whether the received customs declaration documents are complete, correct, valid, and whether the content is consistent. If the documents reviewed comply with national laws and regulations, and the documents submitted for inspection are complete and correct, the customs will immediately proceed to inspect the export goods.

### ③Inspect the goods

All imported and exported goods shall be inspected by the customs, except for those specially approved by the General Administration of Customs.

Inspection means that the customs accepts the declaration of the declaration unit and has audited the declaration unit as the basis, and conducts actual verification of the export goods to determine whether the content declared in the customs declaration documents is consistent with the actual imported and exported goods. .

The purpose of the inspection is to check whether the content reported in the customs declaration documents is consistent with the actual arrival of the goods, whether there is any misreporting, omission, concealment, false reporting, etc., and to review whether the import and export of goods is legal.

### ④ Handle taxation

Collection of export taxes is one of the basic duties of customs. Since the collection of export taxes will inevitably increase the cost of exported goods and affect their competitiveness in the international market, many countries do not levy export taxes on most of their exported goods. At present, the goods subject to export tax in our country include textiles, clothing, etc., which should be subject to export tax according to regulations. After the export enterprise or its agent pays the customs at the prescribed tax rate or provides appropriate guarantees, the customs can sign and seal the release.

⑤ Customs clearance and release

Customs clearance and release is the last business procedure for customs to supervise export goods.

For the customs declaration of import and export goods, after reviewing the customs declaration documents, inspecting the actual goods, and going through the formalities of tax collection or tax reduction and exemption, the owner of the goods or his agent can sign the release seal on the relevant documents. Pick up or ship goods. At this point, the customs supervision of import and export goods is considered to be over.

⑥ Clearance

Customs clearance refers to the goods that need to continue to be subject to follow-up management after being released by the port. The customs will conduct inspections within the specified time limit, and deal with the goods that need to be levied and paid taxes until the customs supervision is completely completed.

Importance: Customs declaration is one of the ties of import and export trade, and an important part of the country's foreign economic and trade activities and international trade chains.

Participants: logistics documents

Related costs:

Customs declaration fees include: freight + insurance; customs duty, value-added tax (charged according to customs and national tax rate regulations); import approval documents and Chinese labels and other fees (not all commodities); port and import formalities fees.

Inspection fee: Customs does not charge fees for inspections in the supervision area. If customs marks are applied to containers, container trucks or other goods, the seal fee will be charged in accordance with regulations. The cost of moving, unpacking or repackaging imported and exported goods arising from the inspection shall be borne by the consignee and consignor of the imported and exported goods. For inspection of goods outside the Customs supervision area, the consignee or consignor of the imported or exported goods or its agent shall pay the Customs a fixed fee.

Matters needing attention: truthful declaration is required. False import and export declarations refer to the fact that the declaration by the customs declaration unit constitutes a violation of customs regulations, but does not have the intention of smuggling. If the import and export commodity name, HS code, quantity, specification, price, trade method, origin and other declaration elements are inconsistent with the actual situation of the goods, it will affect the customs supervision order, tax collection, and trade statistics, and will be subject to customs administrative penalties.
